Category: Chest Wound / Pneumothorax

- Call 911 immediately.
- Seal a sucking chest wound immediately with a chest seal or plastic wrap.
- Apply 3-sided occlusive seal on exhale: tape three sides, leave one open.
- Help person into position of easiest breathing — sitting or semi-reclined.
- If person worsens with seal in place, lift unsealed side to release trapped air.
- Do not leave a sucking chest wound open and unsealed.
- Do not apply a fully airtight seal without a valve — trapped air causes tension pneumothorax.
