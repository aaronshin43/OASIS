Category: Bites and Stings

- Call 911 for signs of anaphylaxis, known venomous bite, or multiple stings.
- Snake bite: keep the person still, immobilize the bitten limb below heart level, remove jewelry — do not cut or suck the venom, it is ineffective and dangerous.
- Bee or wasp sting: scrape the stinger sideways with a card, wash the area with water.
- Tick: grasp with fine tweezers close to the skin, pull straight out steadily — do not crush or squeeze the tick body.
- Animal or dog bite: wash the wound thoroughly with soap and water for 5 minutes; seek medical care for all animal bites.
