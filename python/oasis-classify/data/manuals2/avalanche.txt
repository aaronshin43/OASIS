Category: Avalanche Survival

- If caught: discard poles and skis; use swimming motion toward surface and side.
- As snow slows, cover face with arms and create an air pocket before snow sets.
- Spit — saliva falls toward down; dig in the opposite direction.
- Push one arm toward surface as a marker for rescuers.
- Stay calm, breathe slowly; do not shout unless rescuers are directly above.
- Do not panic and thrash after snow stops — this compresses your air space rapidly.
- Do not dig straight down toward a buried person — approach from below.
