Category: Lost in Wilderness

- STOP: stop moving immediately; panic causes poor decisions and burns energy.
- Think and observe: recall last known position, landmarks, terrain, and supplies.
- Stay put unless in immediate danger — rescuers search known trails first.
- Build shelter if night or rain is approaching — exposure kills before dehydration.
- Signal in groups of three: three whistle blasts, three fires in a triangle.
- Do not keep moving when lost — you will cover more ground to search.
- Do not ignore shelter in favor of food or water — hypothermia is the greatest immediate threat.
