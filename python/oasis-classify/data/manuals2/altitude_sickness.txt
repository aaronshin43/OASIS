Category: Altitude Sickness

- Stop ascending and rest if symptoms are mild: headache, nausea, fatigue.
- Descend immediately if confusion, loss of coordination, or breathlessness at rest.
- Descend at least 500-1000 meters — most effective treatment.
- Give supplemental oxygen if available at 2-4 liters per minute.
- Dexamethasone 8 mg for severe AMS or HACE; Nifedipine 30 mg for HAPE.
- Do not continue ascending while experiencing any altitude sickness symptoms.
- Do not take sleeping pills — they suppress breathing and worsen hypoxia.
